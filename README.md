Top 10 Insights from the HR Analytics Dashboard
* Overall Attrition Rate is Significant (16.12%)

Out of 1,470 employees, 237 employees left, resulting in a 16.12% attrition rate, which is higher than the typical HR benchmark (10–12%).

* Majority of Attrition Occurs Among Employees Aged 25–34

112 employees aged 25–34 left the company — the highest among all age groups.
This suggests mid-career professionals face the most challenges or dissatisfaction.

* Attrition is Higher Among Male Employees

Male attrition: 150

Female attrition: 87
Males represent 63% of total attrition, indicating gender-specific engagement issues.

* Education Background with the Highest Attrition: Life Sciences

Employees with Life Sciences degrees account for 89 attritions, the largest among all education groups, followed by:

Medical: 63

Marketing: 35

This may indicate skill mismatch or higher external opportunities in these fields.

* Sales Department Has the Highest Attrition (56.12%)

Sales accounts for 133 attritions — more than half of total attrition.
Sales is significantly more volatile than HR (12) and R&D (92).

* Employees Under Age 25 Show Low Attrition (38 total)

Younger employees seem more stable or less likely to leave compared to mid-career groups.

* High Concentration of Employees in Age Range 27–36

Histogram shows employee counts peak between ages 27–36, with bins reaching 200+ employees.
This age group forms the core workforce.

* Job Roles with High Total Employee Count Also Show Higher Satisfaction Spread

For example:

Sales Executive → 326 employees, majority rating satisfaction 3 and 4, still experiencing high attrition.
This suggests workload or pressure rather than dissatisfaction may be the cause.

* Advanced Degree Holders (e.g., Technical) Show Lower Attrition

Technical degrees have far fewer attritions (32), despite being a sizeable employee group—indicating more stable career paths.

* Senior Age Groups Show Lowest Attrition

Employees aged 55+ have only 11 attritions.
This suggests higher job stability for senior professionals nearing retirement or long-tenured employees.
